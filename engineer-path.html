Eight modules

How the new libraries fit

OSSU Computer Science: the long-term academic backbone and prerequisite map. It is roughly a two-year, 20-hours/week curriculum, so it is a depth library, not an exam prerequisite.

CS Video Courses: alternate university lectures when a concept needs a second visual explanation.

30 Seconds of Code: quick concept-plus-example refreshers while coding, not a complete curriculum.

Project Based Learning: project ideas once the required concept and tool have been taught.

AI Engineering From Scratch: the deeper AI lane. Its lesson pattern of narrative + runnable code + shipped output is adopted here; phases 11, 13, 14, 17, and 19 become useful after programming foundations.

Technical cofounder literacy

By the end, you should be able to ask for evidence about:

  • Architecture: why this design, what simpler option was rejected, and which constraint drove it?
  • Delivery: show a shipped system, tests, review history, incidents, and what changed afterward.
  • Security: where secrets live, who has access, backups, dependency risks, and the threat model.
  • Reliability: service objectives, monitoring, failure modes, rollback, and on-call ownership.
  • Economics: current infrastructure cost, cost per user/job, scale risks, and build-versus-buy decisions.
  • Leadership: how technical debt is prioritized, how estimates are communicated, and how engineers are hired and reviewed.
